18. CONFLICT MODEL (UPDATED)
==================================================

Conflict must be evaluated with dimension awareness.

---

Complementarity Principle:

Preferences that can be combined MUST NOT be treated as conflicting.

---

Conflict Compatibility Rule:

A conflict may only be created if:

- preferences refer to the SAME dimension
AND
- they are incompatible

---

If preferences refer to DIFFERENT dimensions:

→ they MUST NOT be treated as conflicting

---

If dimension is missing:

→ the system must NOT assume conflict by default

---

Result:

Conflict detection must be dimension-aware.

24. GUARDS (UPDATED)
==================================================

Global Guard Priority Order:

1. has_conflict
2. needs_validation
3. solution_found
4. fallback

---

has_conflict:

Definition:

len(ctx.conflicts where status == "open") > 0

---

Constraint:

Conflicts must only exist if Conflict Compatibility Rule is satisfied.

---

solution_found:

A solution exists based on aggregated preferences.

---

NEW:

solution_complete:

A solution is considered complete if:

- no conflicts exist
- no critical participants missing
- AND no critical dimensions missing (if detectable)

---

Important:

solution_complete is NOT a blocking guard.

It may influence:
- clarification
- confirmation_required
- decision_quality

27. TRANSITION TABLE (CLARIFIED)
==================================================

AGGREGATING:

ON AGGREGATION_COMPLETED:

IF has_conflict:
→ RESOLVING

ELSE IF needs_validation:
→ VALIDATING

ELSE IF solution_found:
→ DECIDING

ELSE:
→ COLLECTING

---

Note:

False conflicts MUST NOT be generated across dimensions.

29. AGGREGATION SEMANTICS (UPDATED)
==================================================

Aggregation must:

- combine compatible preferences across dimensions
- NOT collapse them into a single flat value

---

Example:

Input:
- Wednesday (day)
- Evening (time)

→ composite candidate:
  Wednesday evening

---

NEW:

Aggregation Completeness Rule:

A candidate may be:

- compatible
- but incomplete

---

If required dimensions are missing:

→ candidate is incomplete

---

System must:

- NOT treat as conflict
- NOT finalize immediately
- trigger clarification if necessary

---

==================================================
30. MISSING DIMENSION HANDLING (NEW)
==================================================

The system may detect:

missing_dimension = true

IF:

- preferences exist
- but do not form a coherent candidate
- AND at least one dimension is incomplete

---

Behavior:

IF missing_dimension == true:

→ DO NOT finalize immediately
→ DO NOT create conflict
→ initiate clarification

---

==================================================
31. DIMENSION RELEVANCE (NEW)
==================================================

A dimension is only required if:

- it is necessary to form a valid decision
OR
- it is defined or confirmed by the initiator

---

The system must NOT assume unnecessary dimensions.

---

==================================================
32. INITIATOR CLARIFICATION (UPDATED)
==================================================

When dimensional ambiguity affects decision framing:

System MAY ask initiator:

Example:
"Do you want to decide both day and time?"

---

Constraints:

- non-blocking
- only when ambiguity is material
- must not delay simple decisions

--------------------------------------------------
41.16 DIMENSION MODEL (FUTURE)
--------------------------------------------------

The system may explicitly model decision dimensions.

---

Definition:

A dimension represents an independent aspect of a decision.

---

Examples:

- time (day, time)
- location
- budget
- category

---

Capabilities:

- explicit dimension tracking
- deterministic completeness detection
- structured aggregation output

---

Interaction:

- dimensions may be proposed by system
- must be confirmable by initiator
- must remain flexible (no fixed schema required)

---

Constraints:

- must not reduce generality
- must not require predefined taxonomy

---

Goal:

Improve:

- aggregation quality
- completeness detection
- decision structure
